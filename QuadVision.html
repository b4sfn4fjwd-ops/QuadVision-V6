Why completing the square works

Every quadratic ax² + bx + c hides a perfect square. The x² term is a literal square of side x. The bx term is a strip of width b you can split in half and wrap around two sides of that square.

Wrapping leaves one empty corner. Fill it with a small (b⁄2)² square and the whole shape becomes a clean square of side (x + b⁄2). That single idea turns any quadratic into (x + b⁄2)² = a number — and from there, x is one square-root away.

It is also where the quadratic formula comes from: do this with letters instead of numbers and x = (−b ± √(b² − 4ac)) ⁄ 2a falls straight out.
